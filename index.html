Research

I'm interested in reinforcement learning (RL)-based control for locomotion and legged robots, with a focus on exploring techniques such as imitation learning, policy optimization, sim-to-real transfer, and accelerated learning. I’m also exploring computer vision for perception tasks and embedded electronics for hardware integration in robotic systems.

Visual Locomotion for Legged Robots on Uneven Terrain
Remote Research Intern / Incoming Visiting Researcher, MARMOT Lab, NUS
Advised by Prof. Harold Soh

Developing a robust visual locomotion policy for quadruped robots to navigate challenging, unstructured environments. This research extends the principles of Rapid Motor Adaptation (RMA) by training a control policy that adapts in real-time to terrain features perceived through an onboard camera, leveraging techniques from deep reinforcement learning and computer vision.

Residual Dynamics Prediction for Quadrotors Using Diffusion Models
Research Intern (Summer 2025), DACAS Lab, Indian Institute of Science (IISc)
Advised by Prof. Suresh Sundaram

Developed a novel framework for predicting residual dynamics in quadrotors using diffusion models. This project aimed to improve flight controller performance by learning and compensating for unmodeled aerodynamic effects, leading to more accurate trajectory tracking even with aggressive maneuvers.

Embedded Systems for Electric Vehicle Prototypes
Electronics Intern, Thryv Mobility, IIT Madras Research Park

Contributed to the development of an electric vehicle prototype by designing, fabricating, and testing custom Printed Circuit Boards (PCBs). Gained hands-on experience with embedded systems programming, analog and digital circuit design, and debugging hardware for motor controllers and battery management systems.
